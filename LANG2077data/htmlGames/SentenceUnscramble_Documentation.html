üìç Project Location

Rural Primary School, Henan Province, China

This service-learning project took place in a rural primary school serving students from Grades 1-6, located in Henan Province, one of China's most populous and historically significant regions.

Educational Context and Challenges

The rural primary school faces several significant educational challenges that directly impact student learning outcomes:

  • Limited Specialized Teachers: The school lacks teachers with specialized training in subjects like music, art, and STEM. Many teachers are required to teach multiple subjects outside their primary expertise.

  • Curriculum Gaps: Difficulty in providing comprehensive education across all subject areas, particularly in arts and technology-related subjects.
  • Student Engagement: Traditional teaching methods sometimes struggle to maintain student interest and engagement, especially in subjects perceived as less practical.

2. Our Approach: Using AI to Generate HTML Games

ü§ñ The AI-Powered Development Process

Our approach to creating educational games leverages generative AI to streamline development while ensuring pedagogical effectiveness and cultural relevance. This innovative methodology represents a key learning outcome of the LANG 2077 course, demonstrating human-AI collaboration in educational technology development.

Core Methodology

Human-AI Collaborative Design: Rather than replacing human expertise, AI serves as a powerful tool that amplifies our ability to create customized educational content quickly and effectively.

Step-by-Step Development Process

1 Needs Assessment and Community Engagement

We conducted extensive interviews with local teachers and school administrators to identify specific educational gaps. For English language learning, we discovered that:

  • Students struggled with English grammar and sentence structure
  • Teachers needed interactive tools to teach sentence construction effectively
  • Existing resources were not engaging enough for young English learners
2 Prompt Engineering for Game Design

Using large language models (LLMs), we crafted detailed prompts that specified:

  • Educational Objectives: Clear learning outcomes aligned with primary school English curriculum
  • Target Audience: Age-appropriate content for Grades 2-6 students
  • Cultural Context: Integration of familiar daily scenarios and local cultural references in sentence examples
  • Technical Requirements: HTML5, JavaScript, responsive design, accessibility features
  • Game Mechanics: Interactive elements, immediate feedback, progressive difficulty
3 AI-Generated Code Iteration

The AI generated initial HTML/JavaScript code based on our prompts. We then:

  • Reviewed and refined the generated code for educational effectiveness
  • Tested game mechanics with sample content
  • Iteratively improved prompts to address identified issues
  • Ensured code quality, browser compatibility, and performance
4 Human Refinement and Localization

Critical human intervention included:

  • Pedagogical Review: Ensuring learning objectives are effectively met
  • Cultural Adaptation: Adjusting content to resonate with local students
  • User Experience Testing: Testing with target age groups and refining interface
  • Accessibility Enhancement: Adding features for diverse learning needs
5 Community Feedback Integration

We collected feedback from:

  • Local teachers on curriculum alignment
  • Students during pilot testing sessions
  • School administrators on resource sustainability

This feedback informed final refinements and ensured the game truly addressed community needs.

üí° Key Advantages of AI-Assisted Development

  • Speed: Rapid prototyping allows for multiple iterations and testing cycles
  • Customization: Easy adaptation to specific educational needs and cultural contexts
  • Consistency: AI helps maintain coding standards and best practices
  • Scalability: Process can be replicated for other subjects and grade levels
  • Learning: Students develop skills in prompt engineering and human-AI collaboration

  • CILO 2: Critical evaluation of human-AI interactions, recognizing both capabilities and limitations of AI-generated content

3. The Game: SentenceUnscramble

üéÆ Game Overview

SentenceUnscramble is an English language learning game that challenges students to rearrange scrambled words into correct sentences. The game helps students develop grammar skills, vocabulary understanding, and sentence structure comprehension. It provides immediate feedback and progressive difficulty levels to support students at different English proficiency levels.

Educational Objectives

  • Improve English grammar and sentence structure understanding
  • Enhance vocabulary recognition and usage
  • Develop reading comprehension skills
  • Build confidence in English language learning

Game Features

  • Interactive Word Arrangement: Drag-and-drop or click-to-arrange word tiles
  • Visual Feedback: Colorful, age-appropriate interface with clear word tiles
  • Progressive Difficulty: Adapts from simple 3-word sentences to complex structures
  • Immediate Feedback: Real-time validation of sentence correctness
  • Grammar Reinforcement: Explains why sentences are correct or incorrect

4. Step-by-Step Demo: How SentenceUnscramble Works

This section provides a detailed walkthrough of how students interact with SentenceUnscramble, illustrating the game mechanics and learning progression.

1 Game Launch and Welcome Screen

When students first open SentenceUnscramble, they are greeted with a colorful welcome screen featuring:

  • Scrambled word tiles displayed in random order
  • Clear, simple instructions in both Chinese and English
  • An answer area where students build their sentences

The interface uses large, colorful word buttons and clear visual cues to ensure accessibility for primary school students.

2 Level Selection

Students can practice with different sentence difficulty levels:

  • Beginner Level: Simple 3-4 word sentences (e.g., "I like books")
  • Intermediate Level: 5-6 word sentences with basic grammar (e.g., "She plays piano very well")
  • Advanced Level: Complex sentences with multiple clauses (e.g., "The students are learning English grammar")

This progressive structure allows students to learn at their own pace and build confidence gradually.

3 Interactive Learning Activities

The game includes various interactive activities:

  • Word Selection: Students click on scrambled words to add them to their sentence
  • Sentence Building: Students arrange words in the correct grammatical order
  • Grammar Practice: Students learn proper sentence structure through trial and feedback
  • Vocabulary Reinforcement: Students see and use words in context, improving retention
4 Immediate Feedback System

The game provides instant feedback for every action:

  • Correct Answers: Visual celebration (confetti, stars) and encouraging sounds
  • Incorrect Answers: Gentle correction with hints and explanations
  • Progress Tracking: Visual progress bars and score displays
  • Encouragement: Positive messages that motivate continued learning

This feedback system ensures students understand their mistakes and feel motivated to continue learning.

5 Adaptive Difficulty

The game automatically adjusts difficulty based on student performance:

  • If a student consistently answers correctly, the game introduces more challenging concepts
  • If a student struggles, the game provides additional practice and simpler examples
  • This ensures optimal learning for each individual student
6 Completion and Rewards

Upon completing activities, students receive:

  • Virtual badges and achievements
  • Unlockable content (new songs, instruments, levels)
  • Summary of concepts learned
  • Options to replay or advance to next level

üéØ Learning Progression Example

Session 1 (Beginner): Student practices simple 3-word sentences (e.g., "I like books").

Session 2 (Beginner): Student builds 4-5 word sentences with basic grammar (e.g., "The cat sits on the mat").

Session 3 (Intermediate): Student constructs sentences with adverbs and adjectives (e.g., "She plays piano very well").

Session 4 (Advanced): Student unscrambles complex sentences with multiple clauses and grammar structures.

This progression builds foundational English grammar knowledge while maintaining student engagement through gamification.

6. Reflection and Limitations

ü§î Project Reflection

This project provided valuable learning experiences for both HKBU students and the rural primary school community. Reflecting on the development and implementation process reveals several key insights about human-AI collaboration in educational technology.

‚úÖ Successes and Achievements

What Worked Well

  • Rapid Prototyping: AI-assisted development allowed us to create and test multiple game versions quickly, enabling iterative improvement based on feedback
  • Community Engagement: The needs assessment process ensured the game addressed real educational gaps identified by local teachers
  • Student Engagement: The gamified approach successfully captured and maintained student interest, even for students who typically struggled with music concepts
  • Cultural Adaptation: Bilingual interface and culturally relevant content made the game accessible to the target audience
  • Sustainability: The self-contained HTML format allows the game to be used independently, providing long-term value to the school
  • Skill Development: HKBU students gained practical experience in prompt engineering, human-AI collaboration, and educational technology design

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations and Challenges

Technical Limitations

  • Internet Dependency (Initial): While the game can work offline once downloaded, initial deployment required internet access, which can be unreliable in rural areas
  • Device Compatibility: The game was optimized for desktop browsers, but some students accessed it on tablets with varying screen sizes, requiring responsive design adjustments
  • Browser Support: Older browsers in the school's computer lab sometimes had compatibility issues with advanced JavaScript features
  • Language Complexity: Some English sentences may be too complex for younger students, requiring teacher guidance

Pedagogical Limitations

  • Limited Personalization: While the game adapts difficulty, it cannot fully replace the nuanced feedback and personal attention of a trained English teacher
  • Assessment Gaps: The game focuses on sentence structure but cannot assess more complex language abilities like creative writing or oral communication
  • Social Learning: While peer collaboration emerged naturally, the game itself is primarily individual-focused and doesn't explicitly facilitate group language practice activities
  • Cultural Depth: While we attempted cultural adaptation, the game may not fully capture the depth of local language learning needs and preferences

Implementation Challenges

  • Time Constraints: The 2.5-week intensive service period limited the time available for extensive testing and refinement
  • Language Barriers: Some technical concepts were difficult to explain in simple language appropriate for primary students
  • Teacher Training: Limited time to train teachers on optimal ways to integrate the game into their curriculum
  • Resource Access: Not all students had equal access to devices, requiring scheduling and sharing arrangements

üîÆ Future Improvements and Recommendations

Potential Enhancements

  • Offline-First Design: Develop a Progressive Web App (PWA) version that works seamlessly offline
  • Mobile Optimization: Create a dedicated mobile version optimized for tablets and smartphones commonly used by students
  • Multiplayer Features: Add collaborative elements allowing students to work together on sentence construction
  • Teacher Dashboard: Develop analytics tools for teachers to track student progress and identify learning needs
  • Content Expansion: Add more sentence types, vocabulary themes, and grammar concepts based on curriculum requirements
  • Accessibility Features: Enhance support for students with different learning needs and abilities
  • Local Context Integration: Incorporate more sentences relevant to students' daily lives and local culture
  • Parent Engagement: Create a version that parents can use at home to support their children's English learning

üìö Lessons Learned

Key Takeaways

  • Human-AI Collaboration is Essential: AI accelerates development, but human expertise in pedagogy, cultural understanding, and user experience is irreplaceable
  • Community Input is Critical: The most successful features were those directly requested or validated by local teachers and students
  • Iteration is Key: Multiple rounds of testing and refinement significantly improved the game's effectiveness
  • Simplicity Wins: The most engaging features were often the simplest ones that students could immediately understand and use
  • Sustainability Matters: Designing for long-term use (offline capability, easy updates) ensures lasting impact
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Successful educational technology must respect and integrate local cultural contexts
